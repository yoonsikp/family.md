# Memory Items

#### Cardiac Arrest – ACLS

* Epinephrine
  * Adults: 1 mg IV/IO every 3–5 min
  * Pediatrics: 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO

#### Seizure Management

* Acute Seizure
  * Lorazepam: 4 mg SL/IV (0.1 mg/kg)
  * Midazolam: 5 mg IM/IV (0.2 mg/kg)

* Status Epilepticus (>5 minutes)
  * Levetiracetam: 20 mg/kg IV (infuse slowly; may repeat dosing q12h)
  * Phenytoin / Fosphenytoin (Dilantin): 15–20 mg/kg IV
    *Avoid in pregnancy; infuse over 10–20 minutes — watch for hypotension and arrhythmias*

#### Bradycardia (Symptomatic)

* Atropine: 1 mg IV bolus
  *(Repeat every 3–5 min to max 3 mg)*


#### Tachyarrhythmias

* VF/VT (Pulseless):
  * Amiodarone: 300 mg IV bolus (may repeat 150 mg)
* Stable Narrow Complex SVT:
  * Adenosine: 6 mg rapid IV bolus
    *(If ineffective, give 12 mg)*

#### Myocardial Infarction Consideration

* Nitroglycerin is contraindicated in right-sided or inferior MI

#### Procedural Sedation

* Ketamine: 0.5–1 mg/kg IV
  *(Analgesia & dissociation; avoid in <3 months old)*
* Propofol: 0.5–1 mg/kg IV
  *(Sedation; monitor airway and hemodynamics)*
* Ketofol: 1:1 ketamine + propofol mixture
